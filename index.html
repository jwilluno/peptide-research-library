Certificate of Analysis & verification

Every lot ships with third-party analytical documentation. Here's how to read and verify it.

01

What a COA is

An independent lab report on a specific lot — identity, purity by HPLC, and mass confirmation. It's evidence, not marketing.

02

Match the lot

The lot number printed on the vial matches the lot number on its COA. If they don't match, the document doesn't describe that vial.

03

Check purity & identity

Look for the compound name, the HPLC purity percentage, and a mass-spec identity confirmation. Reputable labs list method and accreditation.

04

Verify at the source

When the issuing lab offers an online lookup or access code, use it to confirm the report is genuine. A current lot COA is available on request.
